# Problem Statement

## Problem Overview

Campus activity management is fragmented, chaotic, and lacks infrastructure. Clubs, events, and operations are coordinated through scattered WhatsApp groups, Google Sheets, and manual processes with no centralization or accountability.

## Who Experiences This Problem?

1. **Club Leads** — Struggle to coordinate activities and communicate with members
2. **Event Organizers** — Manual tracking of RSVPs, check-ins, vendors, and logistics
3. **Institute Administration** — No visibility into campus activity ecosystem
4. **Sponsors** — Limited ways to engage and measure ROI
5. **Participants** — Fragmented discovery and participation channels

## Current Situation

Campus activities are managed using:
- **WhatsApp groups** with scattered messages (lost context, coordination chaos)
- **Google Sheets** with inconsistent data entry and duplication
- **Email chains** for vendor and sponsor communication
- **Manual tracking** of attendance and event execution
- **No audit trail** or institutional memory

## Pain Points

1. **Coordination Chaos** — Information scattered across multiple platforms
2. **Manual Operations** — Hours spent on logistics instead of innovation
3. **Data Inconsistency** — No single source of truth
4. **Vendor Fragmentation** — Contacting vendors via phone/email with no centralization
5. **No Analytics** — Can't measure event success or club health
6. **Scaling Fails** — System breaks at 100+ events/year
7. **Knowledge Loss** — No institutional memory of past events/decisions
8. **Lack of Accountability** — No tracking of task completion or responsibilities

## Impact & Consequences

- **Time:** 10+ hours/week per organizer on coordination instead of strategy
- **Cost:** Vendors contacted multiple times, duplicate expenses, resource waste
- **Quality:** Poor event experiences, missed sponsorship opportunities
- **Opportunity:** Can't identify trends, scale clubs, or grow the ecosystem

## Why This Matters

Campus activities are critical for:
- Student engagement and community building
- Skill development and networking
- Cultural expression and diversity
- Sponsorship and career opportunities

Without proper infrastructure, the campus thrives less, and opportunities are missed.

## Problem Statement (Summary)

> Campus activity management lacks a centralized platform, forcing clubs, organizers, and administrators to coordinate through fragmented tools, resulting in wasted time, missed opportunities, and inability to scale.
